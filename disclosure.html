A Note on How We Keep the Lights On

Corgi Hub uses Amazon affiliate links. That is how this site is funded. Here is what that means in plain terms, because you deserve to know.

The Honest Explanation

When you click a product link in one of our guides and buy something from Amazon, Corgi Hub earns a small commission. Amazon pays it — not you. Your price is identical to what you'd pay walking straight to Amazon.com. The commission is Amazon's cost of doing business with referring sites like this one.

That income covers hosting, the time that goes into writing and reviewing guides, and whatever it costs to keep this thing running. It's a standard arrangement for content sites. We're disclosing it because we think you should know.

What It Does Not Change

Our writing decisions. When the Corgi Hub Writing Desk writes a guide about, say, corgi harnesses, the products we mention are chosen because they matter for a Pembroke Welsh Corgi — not because the commission rate is better on one brand than another. If a product is worth mentioning but has no affiliate link, it gets mentioned anyway. If a product has an affiliate link but isn't actually useful, it doesn't get mentioned.

No company pays Corgi Hub to be featured, reviewed, or described favourably in any guide. That has never happened and is not something we would agree to.
