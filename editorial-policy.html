Our Review Process

The Corgi Hub Writing Desk has a simple standard: don't publish something we wouldn't want to rely on ourselves. This page explains how we get from a topic idea to a guide we're prepared to put our name on.

Starting with What Corgi People Actually Need

Corgi Hub's topics are chosen based on what Pembroke Welsh Corgi owners actually search for, ask about, and struggle with. Not what ranks well for generic dog terms, and not what fills a content calendar. The Writing Desk pays attention to the actual questions corgi owners ask — about ear development, herding instinct in the home, weight management for a breed that will eat forever if you let it — and builds guides from those real needs.

Research and Drafting

Research draws on published veterinary guidance, Pembroke Welsh Corgi Club breed health information, and established sources in canine behavioural science. Where AI writing tools are used in the drafting stage, the Writing Desk treats the output as a first draft that needs substantial editing and verification — not as a finished product. The final article is what the Desk stands behind, not the tools used along the way.

The Desk Review

Before a guide is published, a second member of the Writing Desk reviews it. The reviewer is specifically looking for three things: (1) whether the information applies correctly to the Pembroke (not just corgis generally, and not just herding dogs generally), (2) whether anything could be misread in a way that leads to a bad decision, and (3) whether the guide actually answers the question it promises to answer. If any of those tests fail, the guide goes back for revision.

Updates and Corrections

Corgi Hub updates guides when information changes, when reader corrections are confirmed, or when a periodic review reveals that something has gone stale. We don't leave wrong information on the site to preserve SEO stability. Corrections matter more than rankings. If you find an error, the Contact page is where to send it.

Affiliate Income and Integrity

Corgi Hub earns affiliate income through product links. The disclosure page covers the details. The Writing Desk's review process is not influenced by what earns commissions. An article that is accurate but mentions no affiliatable products goes live without modification. An article that promotes a product for commercial reasons goes in the bin.
