8-16 Weeks: When Corgi Ears Stand Up (Timeline)

Few corgi puppy questions cause as much low-grade panic as the ears. One day they look halfway upright, then teething hits and one ear flops like nothing ever happened. This guide explains what is normal, what is mostly owner folklore, and which signs actually deserve a closer look.

When do corgi ears stand up? There is no single exact week that fits every puppy, but many owners start seeing lift somewhere from roughly 8 weeks to 6 months. Some ears come up early, some go up one at a time, and some seem to make progress, then soften again during teething before settling later.

The useful thing to know here is that the breed standard, puppy development, and owner experience all point in the same general direction, but not to one magic date. The point of this site is to keep those distinctions honest instead of flattening them into fake certainty. Quick answer: corgi ears often begin lifting during the first few months, but teething can make the timeline look messy. One ear up, one ear down, or a temporary droop is usually more normal than first-time owners expect.

Why Owners Care About This So Much

The easiest place to start is the breed itself. The Pembroke Welsh Corgi Club of America breed standard calls for ears that are erect, firm, and medium in size, tapering slightly to a rounded point. That standard is one reason owners watch ear development so closely. They are not making the question up out of nowhere. The breed expectation is built in from the start.

But breed standards describe the end state, not the minute-by-minute puppy timeline. That gap is exactly where confusion begins. People see adult corgis with upright ears, then assume puppies should lock into that look on a fixed schedule. Real puppy development is messier than that.

What the Best Current Evidence Actually Says

There is no single official “all corgi ears stand by X week” document. The best honest answer is an inference from three sources:

  • the breed standard expects erect ears in the finished dog
  • puppy ear cartilage and support muscles keep developing over the first months
  • owner reports repeatedly cluster around the teething window as the messiest phase

Eden Animal Hospital’s puppy ear development guide explains that ears may partially lift around three to five months and settle later, sometimes closer to six or seven months. That is not corgi-only guidance, but it lines up very closely with what corgi owners describe in the wild. The AKC puppy teething timeline matters because teething overlaps with the exact stage where owners often report a previously perky ear drooping again.

So the practical conclusion is not “all ears stand by week 11.” The practical conclusion is that corgi ears often lift during the first several months, and teething can make the process look inconsistent.

Why Teething Gets Blamed So Often

Because owners keep seeing the same pattern. The live Reddit threads around this topic are unusually consistent: a puppy reaches the 3-to-5-month zone, one or both ears had started to perk, and then a droop appears again while the pup is teething. That does not prove a strict medical law, but it does create a strong repeated owner signal.

Examples from recent live threads include puppies with ears still down at 3 months, puppies with one ear tip drooping, and pups whose ears briefly “went backwards” during teething. That is useful because it keeps first-time owners from assuming every floppy phase is a disaster. Important distinction: “teething can make things wobble” is not the same as “ignore everything.” If the ear looks painful, inflamed, or injured, stop treating it as a cute phase and talk to your vet.

What Is More Useful Than Panicking About the Exact Week

The smartest question is not “why isn’t this perfect yet?” It is “does this still look like normal development?” That shifts your attention to pattern rather than impatience. For most puppies, normal development means change over time, not identical progress every week.

Useful things to watch instead of obsessing over the calendar:

  • is there gradual change over the months?
  • is one ear experimenting with lift, even if it does not stay there yet?
  • did the droop happen during the heavy chewing and teething stage?
  • does the ear look healthy and comfortable?

If the answer is mostly yes, waiting is often more productive than spiraling. And if you want broader site navigation while more corgi pages roll out, the blog page is now the cleanest jumping-off point.

Should You Tape a Corgi Puppy’s Ears?

This is where internet advice gets noisy fast. Some owners jump straight to taping because they are afraid of “missing the window.” Others treat any mention of taping like a scam. The better answer is more restrained: do not make taping your default move just because a young puppy’s ears are not perfect yet.

Because the timeline naturally varies, many puppies simply need more time. If you are seriously considering taping, get guidance from someone who knows the breed well, ideally your breeder or veterinarian, instead of improvising with random household materials. The risky part is not just the tape itself. It is the false urgency that makes owners act before they understand what is actually normal.

When It Is Time to Worry Less About the Timeline and More About Health

The biggest mistake owners make is worrying about the wrong thing. Slow ears are usually less concerning than painful ears. If the ear posture is odd but the ear looks healthy, that often points back to development. If the posture changes with scratching, odor, redness, discharge, swelling, or obvious discomfort, the conversation changes completely.

That is why I would worry sooner about:

  1. frequent head shaking
  2. repeated scratching or pawing at the ear
  3. bad odor or discharge
  4. pain when touched
  5. sudden droop after a possible injury

The Most Honest Corgi Ear Timeline Answer

If you want the shortest version after all that nuance, here it is: many corgi ears begin to stand somewhere during the first few months, many go through awkward teething-era reversals, and some simply take longer than owners expect. The breed standard explains the destination. Development explains the detours.

That is why the best answer is not a neat “12 weeks exactly” claim. The best answer is that variation is normal, especially before teething is finished, and the real red flags are pain, infection, or injury rather than a puppy whose ears are still figuring themselves out.

FAQ

When do corgi ears stand up?

Many owners start seeing lift somewhere between roughly 8 weeks and 6 months, but there is no single exact week that fits every puppy.

Is one ear up and one ear down normal?

Yes. That is one of the most common corgi puppy phases and does not automatically mean anything is wrong.

Can teething make a corgi ear droop again?

Yes. That is one of the most repeated owner reports during the puppy stage and is part of why the timeline often looks inconsistent.

Should I tape my corgi’s ears immediately?

No, not automatically. Many puppies simply need more time, and it is smarter to get informed guidance than to act from panic.

When should I stop waiting and call the vet?

If the ear is painful, red, smelly, swollen, discharging, or suddenly changed after injury, treat that as a health issue instead of a timing issue.
